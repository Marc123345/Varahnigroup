THE DAVID MABILU FOUNDATION
Building Stronger Communities Through Opportunity
The David Mabilu Foundation (DMF) is a social development organisation committed to reducing poverty and sustainably improving the living standards of poor and marginalised communities across South Africa.
Through education support, housing initiatives, and community development programmes, the Foundation works to empower individuals and communities to become self-sustaining and independent.
The Foundation was established by David Mabilu with a vision to give back meaningfully to the communities that continue to shape South Africa’s future.
OUR GOAL
The primary goal of the David Mabilu Foundation is to reduce poverty and sustainably improve the living standards of disadvantaged and marginalised communities throughout South Africa.
We believe that lasting change is achieved when communities are empowered with access to education, housing, and opportunities for social and economic development.
HOW WE ACHIEVE OUR GOAL

Through these partnerships, the Foundation is able to extend its reach and ensure that its initiatives deliver meaningful and sustainable impact.
OUR KEY PROGRAMME AREAS
The David Mabilu Foundation focuses on three core programme areas that directly address community needs and long-term development.
Education
Education is a powerful catalyst for breaking the cycle of poverty. Through scholarships, school support programmes, and infrastructure development, the Foundation invests in the future of young South Africans.
Housing
Access to safe and dignified housing is essential for improving the quality of life of vulnerable families. The Foundation supports housing initiatives that provide homes for disadvantaged individuals and families.
Community Upliftment
The Foundation supports community-driven initiatives that strengthen social wellbeing, encourage youth development, and promote economic participation.
WHAT WE DO
The David Mabilu Foundation focuses on initiatives and projects that assist individuals and communities to become self-sustaining and independent.
Through targeted programmes, partnerships, and direct support, the Foundation works to address real community challenges while promoting long-term empowerment.
EDUCATION
The David Mabilu Foundation believes that education is one of the most powerful tools for creating a better future for South African children, particularly those from previously disadvantaged backgrounds.
Since its establishment in 2011, the Foundation has provided scholarships to approximately thirty-four school-going children from primary school through to tertiary education.
These scholarships support learners through:
Full tuition payments
Monthly stipends
School uniforms
Educational trips
Monthly grocery support for families
Beyond individual scholarships, the Foundation also supports schools by providing essential resources and infrastructure.
Beneficiary schools include:
Galananzhele Primary School
Tshifhatani Primary School
Tshidimbini Secondary School
Tshiavha Primary School
These schools have received computers for learners as well as additional classrooms built to create improved learning environments.
COMMUNITY SUPPORT INITIATIVES
The Foundation regularly supports schools, faith-based organisations, and community programmes that contribute to education and social wellbeing.
In 2012, during Nelson Mandela Day, Founder David Mabilu and Vharanani Properties staff spent their 67 minutes giving back at Mambo Primary School in Chiawelo, Soweto — a school Mr. Mabilu attended as a learner. The team painted classrooms, cleaned the school facilities, and donated cleaning and hygiene supplies.
In 2013, the Foundation also supported student financial assistance programmes run by faith-based organisations in Venda, including:
United African Apostolic Church (UAAC)
Church of Christ
HOUSING
The David Mabilu Foundation supports housing initiatives aimed at improving living conditions for disadvantaged families.
Working in partnership with organisations such as Capricorn FM, the Foundation has helped provide housing for vulnerable households.
One such initiative involved the donation of a home to a designated family in Ga-Mphahlele in Limpopo.
The Foundation has also supported Nelson Mandela Day initiatives focused on housing development, working with:
The Department of Human Settlements in Mpumalanga
The Office of the Premier in Limpopo
The Office of the President
Through these collaborations, the Foundation helped provide homes for vulnerable individuals, including the construction of a three-bedroom house for an elderly woman in the village of Eenzaam in Limpopo who had not received her social grant since 1985.
COMMUNITY UPLIFTMENT
The David Mabilu Foundation actively supports community upliftment programmes, particularly those focused on women, youth, and disadvantaged communities.
The Foundation regularly responds to requests for assistance from community organisations and supports initiatives such as:
Youth development programmes
Women’s community initiatives
Local economic development projects
The Foundation also supports sports and arts development programmes within disadvantaged communities.
Over the years, the Foundation has donated:
Sporting kits
Soccer balls
Boots and sports equipment
to schools and community clubs.
These initiatives encourage youth participation in sports, teamwork, and positive community engagement.
In 2017, the Foundation also responded to a community request in the Free State by donating blankets to vulnerable members of the community during winter.
Additionally, during Women’s Day 2017, the Foundation honoured elderly women in Chiawelo by providing blankets and hosting a day of appreciation dedicated to them.
